What This System Does

The OMS is your digital command center for taking orders, tracking progress, managing the menu, and keeping customers organized. Every action automatically updates the backend — nothing falls through the cracks.

🔒
Important rule You are the source of truth. All changes happen through this app — not directly in Google Sheets or ClickUp. Manual edits to Sheets cause data to go out of sync.

App Tabs

📊 Dashboard
Active orders, revenue, pipeline status, and blast announcements
📋 Orders
Kanban board showing all orders by status — your main working view
➕ New Order
Add a new customer order manually
🍽 Menu
View and add menu items
👤 Customers
Profiles, order history, and tags
🛒 Grocery
Auto-generated shopping list from confirmed orders
⚙️ Settings
Admin-only — staff do not need this tab

Taking a New Order

Use this when a customer contacts you directly (text, call, DM). Go to + New Order in the top navigation.

What to fill in

FieldWhat to enter
NameCustomer's full name — type to search existing, or enter new
PhoneWith area code, e.g. 4045550100
EmailOptional but recommended — needed for email receipts
Pickup DateThe date they plan to pick up
Pickup TimeSelect from 11:00 AM – 7:00 PM dropdown
ItemsClick + to add each item and quantity
TaxLeave at 0% unless required — ask Jeffrey if unsure
Payment MethodCash App / Zelle / Stripe / Cash / PayPal
Payment StatusUnpaid / Deposit Paid / Paid In Full

What NOT to do

  • Do not enter a fake or test name — all orders go to Google Sheets and ClickUp
  • Do not leave phone number blank — it's required for customer records
  • Do not change the tax rate unless Jeffrey has approved it

Submitting

  1. Review the subtotal and total at the bottom
  2. Click Add to Workflow
  3. Confirmation toast appears — order is on the Kanban board
⚡
What happens automatically The order is sent to ClickUp, added to Google Sheets, and (once SMS/email is live) a confirmation text and email go to the customer. You don't need to do anything else.

Managing Orders (Kanban Board)

The Orders tab shows all active orders as cards in columns. Each column is a stage in fulfillment.

Order Statuses

StatusWhat it means
NewOrder taken, not yet confirmed with the customer
ConfirmedCustomer confirmed — order is locked in
In ProgressRhi is cooking this order
ReadyPackaged and ready for pickup — customer gets notified
CompletedCustomer picked up the order

How to update an order status

  1. Click the order card to open the detail view
  2. Change the Order Status dropdown to the new stage
  3. Optionally update Payment Status if they've paid
  4. Click Done — the card moves and ClickUp updates automatically
📋
Ready status is important Marking an order as Ready triggers an automatic text and email to the customer (once Twilio and SendGrid are activated). Only mark Ready when the food is actually packaged and waiting.

Voiding an order

If a customer cancels or an order needs to be removed from revenue:

  1. Open the order card
  2. Click Void Order (red button)
  3. The order stays on record but is excluded from revenue totals

Customer Profiles

Every order creates a customer record. Go to the Customers tab to view profiles.

What you can do

  • Click any customer card to open their profile
  • Tap Email, Text, or Call to contact them directly from the app
  • View full order history and total spent
  • Add tags to segment them (e.g. VIP, Oxtail Regular, Family & Friends)

Adding tags

  1. Open the customer profile
  2. In the Segments / Tags section, start typing a tag name
  3. Select from dropdown suggestions or type a new one
  4. The tag appears on their profile and in the filter bar
🏷
Tags are for organizing, not required Tags help Jeffrey send targeted blasts (e.g. text only VIP customers about an exclusive offer). You don't have to tag every customer, but it helps over time.

Things NOT to do in Customers

  • Do not use the Remove button unless a customer explicitly requests deletion
  • Do not edit phone numbers — they link to order history

The Grocery List

The Grocery tab automatically calculates what you need to buy based on confirmed orders. Use this before every shopping run.

  1. Go to the Grocery tab
  2. Select a specific pickup date, or check All Pending Orders
  3. The shopping list generates automatically from orders in progress
  4. The estimated cost shows on the right
📝
For Rhi The grocery list shows ingredient quantities based on menu item settings. The more accurate the recipe data (ingredients, servings per pan), the more accurate this list will be. Work with Jeffrey to update ingredient data when menu items change.

Blast Announcements

Blasts are mass announcements — used to tell customers about upcoming availability, specials, or order cutoffs. Found on the Dashboard tab.

👤
Who sends blasts Jeffrey handles blasts. Rhi does not need to use this feature.

How Jeffrey sends a blast

  1. Go to Dashboard
  2. Fill in Occasion (e.g. Mother's Day Special)
  3. Set the Order Cutoff date
  4. Write the message
  5. Choose who to send to — All Customers or a specific tag segment
  6. Check SMS, Email, or both
  7. Click Send Blast
⚠️
Once sent, it cannot be unsent Review the message carefully before clicking Send Blast. The text and email go out immediately to every customer in the selected group.

Quick Reference — Daily Checklist

🌅 At the start of every pickup day

  • Open the app and sign in
  • Go to Orders — confirm which orders are expected today
  • Go to Grocery tab — cross-reference your shopping list

📥 When a new order comes in

  • Go to + New Order and fill in all fields
  • Confirm the total with the customer before submitting
  • Click Add to Workflow — done

🍳 As you cook

  • Move orders from Confirmed → In Progress in the app
  • When packaged and ready, move to Ready
  • When picked up, move to Completed

🔧 If something looks wrong

  • Order not appearing? Try clicking Sync to Workflow on the Orders tab
  • Customer data missing? Tell Jeffrey — he can run a force sync from Settings
  • App not loading? Check your internet connection and try refreshing
  • Anything else? Contact Mambay (MK) for technical issues
